The story

Built by parents,
for parents

DoNext started as a family problem. Sunday evening, nothing planned, two kids climbing the walls.

What is DoNext?

Your family's daily ideas engine

DoNext is a curated family events discovery service for UK cities. Every morning, we scour local council pages, museum listings, community groups, Facebook events, and neighbourhood message boards — so you don't have to.

We don't just list what's on. We editorially select the best — weigh them against the week's weather — and deliver them in plain language, straight to your inbox.

Editorial curation, not an algorithm

Real humans read the listings and make real editorial judgements. We're looking for the unexpected — the community forest school, the free museum morning, the pop-up that won't appear on any tourist website.

Weather-smart recommendations

We check the forecast and adjust every morning. Rainy Thursday? Here are five brilliant indoor options. Sunny Sunday? Time to get outside.

Age-appropriate for 0–12

Every event is tagged with the age range that will genuinely enjoy it. We never recommend a toddler group to an 8-year-old, or a forest school to a teenager.

Why we built this

Parents shouldn't have to spend hours finding something to do

The information is out there — scattered across 47 different websites, five council pages, three Facebook groups, and a WhatsApp message someone sent you three weeks ago. No parent has time for that.

DoNext started as a text message between two parents: "what are you doing this weekend?" It turned into a shared spreadsheet. Then a weekly email to twelve friends. Then a city service.

"We believe every family in every UK city deserves to know what's brilliant and free and happening in their neighbourhood this weekend."
